My passion for video games

Growing up, I enjoyed playing video games and would play a wide range of genres, with my favourite being Need for Speed: Most Wanted (2005) on the PlayStation 2. This passion was what led to my interest in games development and wanting to know how games work. Looking at how games work, I have an appreciation for the time developers have spent creating games and would love to continue in my career making games that can inspire others.

I also enjoy building PCs for myself and others and have spent lots of time building, repairing, and upgrading computers, spending a great length of time researching what parts are compatible and ensuring that these builds are able to live up to the ever-changing requirements that software (especially games) have.


Hobbies outside of Games

Outside of gaming, I enjoy practicing Martial Arts.

I have dedicated my Saturday mornings to Shotokan Karate since 2008, where by the time I was 16 I had achieved my 1st degree black belt and had come third in 2 regional tournament events: Individual Kata and Team Kumite. I am always pushing to improve myself, and when I am not preparing for a tournament or grading I have the responsibility to teach and help the lower grades to understand the art.

Another Martial Art I practice is Taekwondo in the University society, where I am the president and on my way to achieve a black belt in the art as well. As president, I have the responsibilities of communicating with external instructors and other university societies, along with being in charge of setting up events for the society.
